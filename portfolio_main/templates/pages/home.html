My Story

This site is my portal to express my views, help others, and show of some my projects I love to work on. It will be full of book reviews, my ways I overcame challenges, and Entrepreneurial thoughts and resources. I hope you are able to find something you like.

I am currently a student whom loves a variety of things. My two biggest passions are technology and economics. I spend the majority of my time programming and learning. I never want to stop learning and a big reason I made this site is to help remember all things I learn. I will be writing a summary and review for all new books I read and several of the great ones I read in the past. I am in the process of documenting the struggles and gratification of entrepreneurship. I have a great passion for these items and I hope that I reach others who share that passion. Feel free to email, tweet or connect with me. I hope you enjoy visit!

Personal Story Timeline

I have a variety of interest and have had wonderful life full of learning. I am happy to finally reach out and tell my own story. Below is a timeline of major personal events over the last year or so.

  • August 2014

    Spent the entire summer learning programming during a 12 week bootcamp. One of the best decisions of my life. I got hooked really fast and now spend most of my time working on side projects while taking classes for my senior year.

  • March 4th 2014

    I entered this competition after seeing my Mom priced gouged by healthcare costs. I came up with an idea in less than a week and put together a SWOT analysis. After making the finals, I found my idea to broad and pivoted to something better. This was the first major award I ever won. I learned an incredible amount in a short time because of it.

  • September 2013

    After working two jobs for long enough, I decided it was time to finish my degree quickly. I picked Saint Mary's because I though I could stand out and I could build great relationships with my professors. I have not been disappointed. I love to have in depth discussions on finance, economics, and in the Seminar program. Saint Mary's is wonderful institution which allowed me to stay in the Bay Area.
